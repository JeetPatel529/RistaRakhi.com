Raksha Bandhan is a cherished Hindu festival celebrated in India. It symbolizes the bond between brothers and sisters, with sisters tying Rakhi threads around their brothers' wrists as a symbol of love and protection. The festival promotes unity, respect, and emotional support among siblings, fostering a sense of togetherness in society. It is a joyous occasion where brothers pledge to protect and support their sisters, and gifts are exchanged as a token of affection. Raksha Bandhan holds immense cultural and emotional significance, strengthening the bond between siblings across the country.
